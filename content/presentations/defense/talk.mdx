# Exponential asymptotic

$$
  \begin{gathered}
    f(\epsilon) = r\epsilon + o(\epsilon)  
    \quad\Leftrightarrow\quad \frac{f(\epsilon)}{\epsilon} \rightarrow r \\[1em]
    \leadsto \quad f(\epsilon) \approx r \epsilon 
    %
    \\[4em]
    f(\epsilon) = \exp\big(-r/\epsilon + o(1/\epsilon)\big)  
    \quad\Leftrightarrow\quad \epsilon \log f(\epsilon) \rightarrow -r \\[1em]
    \leadsto \quad f(\epsilon) \approx \exp\big(-r/\epsilon \big)
  \end{gathered}
$$

# Large deviation principle

Fix <span className="yellow">measures $(p^\epsilon)_{\epsilon>0}$</span> on <span className="orange">topological space $\bbX$</span>.


<Fragment>

\begin{aligned}
  -\inf_{\xi \in \Gamma^\circ} I(\xi)
  &\leq \liminf_{\epsilon\rightarrow0} \epsilon \log p^\epsilon(\Gamma) \\
  &\leq \limsup_{\epsilon\rightarrow0} \epsilon \log p^\epsilon(\Gamma)

# Light-tails assumption

If for each $u \in \bbV$, there exist $\tau > 0$ and $x \in \bbX^\circ$ such that,

$$
\Exp_{\Prb_x}\exp\langle u, X_\tau \rangle < \infty,
$$


<Fragment>
  then $X$ is a special jump-diffusion with affine <span className="green">drift $\beta$</span>, <span className="pink">diffusion $\alpha$</span>, and <span className="sky">jump-kernel $\mu$</span>.
</Fragment>

<Fragment>
$$
\begin{gathered}
  \beta(x) = b_0 + \sum_{i=1}^d x^i b_i, \quad
  \alpha(x) = a_0 + \sum_{i=1}^d x^i a_i, \\
  \mu(x, \rmd v) = m_0(\rmd v) + \sum_{i=1}^d x^i m_i(\rmd v)
\end{gathered}
$$
</Fragment>
