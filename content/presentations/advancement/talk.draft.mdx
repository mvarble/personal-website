## Not an easy problem!

We typically cannot hope to calculate $\mathrm{P}(X \in A)$ explicitly.

<Fragment style={{ display: 'block' }} index={ 0 }>
  <strong className="lightgreen" style={{ display: 'block', textAlign: 'center' }}>Asymptotic approximations</strong>
  <blockquote>
    <Fragment style={{ display: 'block', position: 'relative' }} index={ 2 }>
      <span className="orange">CLTs.</span> 
      families $(X^\epsilon)_{\epsilon > 0}$ have weak convergence $X^\epsilon_\#\mathrm{P} \rightarrow \mu$.
      <div style={{ position: 'relative' }}>
        <div style={{ textAlign: 'center', padding: '0.5em' }}>
          $\mathrm{P}(X \in A) = \mathrm{P}(X^\epsilon \in A) = X^\epsilon_\#\mathrm{P} A  \approx \mu A $
        </div>
        <Fragment out>
          <div style={{ 
            position: 'absolute',

          </div>
        </Fragment>
      </div>
    </Fragment>
    <Fragment style={{ display: 'block', position: 'relative' }}>
      <span className="orange">LDPs.</span>

      <div style={{ position: 'relative' }}>
        <div style={{ textAlign: 'center', padding: '0.5em' }}>
          $\mathrm{P}(X \in A) = \mathrm{P}(X^\epsilon \in A) \approx e^{-r(A)/\epsilon}$
